## 5. Pricing Psychology: One-Time Setup vs. Subscription

### One-Time Setup
**Pros:**
- Lower barrier to entry, easier sale
- Clear deliverable = clear value exchange
- No ongoing obligation for either party
- Higher perceived value per transaction

**Cons:**
- Revenue is lumpy/unpredictable
- No recurring relationship
- Customer may churn and never return
- You're constantly hunting new customers

### Subscription/Ongoing Support
**Pros:**
- Predictable recurring revenue (MRR)
- Higher lifetime value (LTV)
- Builds relationship and lock-in
- Easier to upsell/cross-sell

**Cons:**
- Harder initial sell ("another subscription?!")
- Ongoing obligation to deliver value
- Churn management required

### Psychology Insights:
1. **Anchoring:** Show the one-time price first, then present the subscription as "ongoing peace of mind" at a fraction
2. **Decoy effect:** Three tiers where the middle tier looks like the obvious choice
3. **Loss aversion:** "Without ongoing support, you risk downtime" â€” frame subscription as insurance
4. **Price-quality signal:** Below $100 signals "amateur"; $200+ signals "professional"; $500+ signals "premium"
5. **The $497 effect:** Odd-specific pricing ($197, $297, $497) signals "precisely calculated value" and outperforms round numbers in digital services
6. **Payment pain:** One-time $500 hurts more than $49/month for 12 months ($588 total) â€” subscription can extract more total revenue

### Best practice: **Hybrid model** â€” one-time setup fee + optional ongoing support subscription
